What is covid-19?

COVID-19 is a disease caused by a virus called SARS-COV-2.

How does the virus spread?

covid-19 is though to spread mainly through close contact from person to person, including between people who are physically near each other(within about 6 feet).

What are the symptoms of COVID-19?

Fever, cough, trouble breathing, cold, chills, muscle pain, headache, a loss of taste or smell, vomiting, tiredness etc.

What is community spread?

Community spread means people have been infected with the virus in an area, including some who are not sure how or where they became infected.

How can I protect myself?

By following the things like wear a mask, stay 6 feet away from others, get vaccinated, avoid crowds and poorly ventilated spaces, wash your hands etc.

What should I do if I have had closed contact with someone who has COVID-19?

self-quarantine by staying home for 14 days, if possible stay away from others, Be alert from symptoms of Covid-19.

What are the symptoms and complications that COVID-19 can cause?

Symptoms may appear 2-14 days after exposure to the virus. If you have fever, cough, or other symptoms, you might have COVID-19.

How can I get tested for a current infection and what does my test mean?

It checks specimens from your nose or your mouth to find out if you are currently infected with SARS-CoV-2, the virus that causes COVID-19.

Can someone test negative and later test positive on a viral test for COVIID-19?

Yes, it is possible. You may test negative if the sample was collected early in your infection and test positive later during this illness.

Who should wear a Mask?

All adults and kids older than 2 years should wear a mask when out in public and around others.

corona viruses are viruses that circulate among animals. Some corona viruses can infect humans.
